THE GLOVE AND THE HAND
A glove has the form of a hand.
It is shaped for a hand.
It even looks like a hand.

But without the hand inside it,
it is limp and purposeless.

Adam was the glove —
bearing the shape, designed for the filling,
but not yet containing the life.
THE GLOVE TRIED TO MOVE ON ITS OWN
THE IMAGE MARRED
THEN CAME THE HAND ITSELF
CHRIST — THE TRUE IMAGE
AND NOW — THE HAND ENTERS THE GLOVE
CONFORMED TO THE IMAGE
UNTIL
THE IMAGE COMPLETED

The image of God is not a static attribute man possesses.

It is a trajectory — from shadow to substance,

from vessel to fullness,

from solitary to corporate,

from Adam to Christ expressed in many brethren.
