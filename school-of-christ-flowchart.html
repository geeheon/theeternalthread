THE SCHOOL OF CHRIST

Ephesians 4:20–21

THE FOUNDATION
"But you have not so learned Christ, if indeed you have heard Him and have been taught by Him, as the truth is in Jesus."
EPHESIANS 4:20–21
THE WORDS
mathētēs A learner. One under discipline. Not a student of information — a person being formed.
manthanō To learn — by use, by practice, by experience. Not merely to be told.
emathete "You learned" — aorist. A decisive event. You came under discipline to Christ Himself.
THE CRITICAL DISTINCTION
AUSTIN-SPARKS' OBSERVATION
Jesus said: "Learn of Me."
Paul said: "You did not so learn Christ."

One little word removed — and the whole meaning changes.
Not learning about Him. Not imitating from outside.
But learning the Person — inwardly, by the Spirit,
through experience, through breaking, through revelation.

METAPHOR KEY ▼
🚲 Bicycle — self-effort. The self trying to imitate Christ from outside: outward discipline, behaviour modification, programme-driven formation. The engine is still you.
🚗 Car — Christ Himself. You have been placed inside Him. The School of Christ teaches not about Him from outside, but forms Him within — by the Spirit, through experience.

THE METHOD

A disciple is not one who has completed a course.

A disciple is one who is under the hand of the Teacher —

being broken, being shown, being formed,

until the only thing that remains is Christ.

SUPPORTING VERSES

The person who really does begin to move
is the person who has had his final despair
over himself, and has come to see —
it is "no longer I, but Christ."
